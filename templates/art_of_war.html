The Art of War" is an ancient Chinese military treatise dating from the Late Spring and Autumn Period (roughly 5th century BC). The work, which is attributed to the ancient Chinese military strategist Sun Tzu, is composed of 13 chapters. Each chapter is devoted to a distinct aspect of warfare, making it the definitive work on military strategies and tactics of its time. Its principles have been applied to various fields beyond the military, including business and leadership.

Sun Tzu, also rendered as Sun Zi, was a Chinese general, military strategist, writer, and philosopher who lived in the Eastern Zhou period of ancient China. His work, "The Art of War", has become one of the most influential strategy texts in East Asia and has gained popularity in the West for its insights into strategy and leadership.
